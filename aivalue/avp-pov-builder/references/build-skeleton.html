How to read this dashboard — glossary of financial and methodology terms

Financial terms

P50 / P10–P90
Percentile outcomes from 10,000-trial Monte Carlo simulation. P50 = median scenario; P10–P90 = 80% confidence band.
NPV (Net Present Value)
Discounted cash flows over the horizon at the client's hurdle rate, after effective tax rate.
Hurdle rate
Client WACC plus 2% risk premium for AI transformation portfolios.
Portfolio realization factor
Applied to all benefit-derived metrics (marked *). Calibrated for this industry — delivery experience shows approximately 60% of full-deployment value is captured at portfolio scale.
LOE (Level of Effort)
Quick Win = deployable on current stack in <6 months. Standard Project = 6–12 months. Major Initiative = 12–24 months with platform dependencies.
Relative LOE percentile
0–100 score ranking each use case by implementation difficulty relative to the full broad pool of candidates evaluated.

Slalom & AVP terms

Strategic Goal
A 3–5 year aspirational business outcome that anchors the AI portfolio. Each use case maps to one or more strategic goals.
AVP (AI Value Platform)
Slalom's framework for sizing and sequencing AI use cases against process-level task analysis. This dashboard is built from AVP analysis of the client's business functions.
Broad pool
The full set of ~47 AI candidates evaluated before downselection. The prioritized portfolio of 12 is drawn from this pool; the remainder are evaluated and deprioritized — not ignored.
BENCHMARK / INFERRED
Every financial line is classified. BENCHMARK = drawn from a named public source. INFERRED = derived from related benchmarks with a disclosed reasoning chain. Both are illustrative.

Methodology

How the portfolio was scored

AVP applies a structured six-step process and ten enhanceIQ dimensions to evaluate and rank use cases by strategic value and implementation feasibility.

Step 1
AI Readiness
Assess current process and technology landscape for AI fit
Step 2
Catalog
Map candidate use cases to business functions and goals
Step 3
Opportunity Zones
Cluster by value and feasibility to surface priority areas
Step 4
Integrated Solutions
Design end-to-end architectures across related use cases
Step 5
Current Context
Overlay known constraints, in-flight work, and dependencies
Step 6
Business Cases
Quantify value with NPV, payback, and benefit estimates

enhanceIQ Scoring Dimensions

01
Task Nature

Degree to which the task is structured, rule-based, and amenable to AI execution

02
Work Complexity

Cognitive load, exception handling, and judgment required to complete the work

03
Collaboration Scope

Number of stakeholders, systems, and handoffs involved in the workflow

04
Data Profile

Availability, quality, and accessibility of data needed to train or run the solution

05
Operational Environment

Stability and maturity of the operating context where AI will be deployed

06
Expertise Requirement

Specialist knowledge or domain depth needed to build and sustain the solution

07
Stakeholder Impact

Breadth and depth of change management required across affected roles

08
Regulatory Impact

Compliance, audit, and approval requirements that govern the use case

09
Risk Profile

Consequence of error, failure modes, and reversibility of AI-driven decisions

10
Resource Demand

Engineering effort, infrastructure spend, and ongoing operational cost

Each use case receives a composite enhanceIQ score from 0–100 across these ten dimensions. Scores feed directly into the Value vs. Feasibility matrix on the AI Portfolio tab and drive the Horizon 1 / 2 / 3 phasing sequence.

Explicit go/no-go criteria at each phase boundary. Meeting all criteria before committing the next phase's budget protects against over-investment in an underperforming program.

Gate 1 — Month 3

Phase 0 → Phase 1 launch readiness

Go criteria
  • Data platform MVP live — ingestion pipelines for UC-01 and UC-02 source systems operational
  • AI governance policy approved and model review process defined
  • Phase 1 team confirmed: 2 data engineers, 1 ML engineer, product owner assigned
  • UC-01 and UC-02 use-case charters signed off by function owners
On no-go: Extend Phase 0 by one sprint cycle; reassess at next monthly steering review. Do not commit Phase 1 one-time investment until all criteria met.
Gate 2 — Month 12

Phase 1 → Phase 2 investment commitment

Go criteria
  • UC-01 achieving ≥70% forecast accuracy improvement over baseline in production
  • UC-02 demonstrated ≥15% reduction in unplanned maintenance events over 60-day window
  • Phase 2 budget formally approved by finance; vendor procurement initiated
  • Change management rollout complete for Phase 1 user population (adoption ≥60%)
On no-go: Phase 1 extension with 90-day remediation plan. Root-cause analysis required before Phase 2 investment committed. Deprioritized candidates re-scored against updated context.
Gate 3 — Month 24

Phase 2 → Phase 3 backlog entry

Go criteria
  • UC-03 scrap rate reduction meeting or exceeding target threshold
  • UC-04 design cycle time reduction ≥20% from pre-deployment baseline
  • Platform capacity validated to support Phase 3 agentic workload requirements
  • Deprioritized backlog (UC-05, UC-06) re-scored against latest organization context and data availability
On no-go: Continue Phase 2 optimization for one additional quarter. Phase 3 entry deferred; backlog candidates held for next AVP re-run rather than committed speculatively.

Delivery & Technical Assumptions

Financial Model

  • 5-year benefit horizon; no terminal value

Technical
