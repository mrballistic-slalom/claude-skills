# The Four Winning Patterns

Anchor framework for every Slalom PoV. Sourced from Slalom's 2024 Executive Perspectives research and reinforced by leading consulting research on the 5% of enterprises that capture material AI value.

These four patterns separate AI-value winners from the 60% who spend and see nothing. They apply across processes, industries, and functions — but the *specifics* of how they manifest must be mapped to the topic.

---

## Pattern 1 — Shared platforms

**Principle:** Winners build shared AI/data platforms; laggards run siloed pilots.

**Generic statement:** AI value compounds when models, data, and tooling are shared across the workflow. Siloed pilots produce one-off wins that don't propagate.

**Per-topic mapping examples:**
- *Mortgage Origination:* Unified LOS/POS/data layer instead of channel-by-channel point solutions.
- *Specialty Pharma:* Shared molecule-discovery and trial-design platform across therapeutic areas instead of TA-specific stacks.
- *HR:* Single talent intelligence layer across recruiting, learning, performance, and workforce planning instead of point HRIS modules.

**Citation:** Slalom 2024 Executive Perspectives + leading consulting research.

---

## Pattern 2 — End-to-end workflow redesign

**Principle:** Winners redesign whole workflows; laggards optimize edges.

**Generic statement:** AI value comes from rethinking the workflow around AI, not bolting AI onto existing handoffs. Edge automation produces 3-5% gains; redesign produces 30-50%+.

**Per-topic mapping examples:**
- *Mortgage Origination:* Originate-to-close-to-servicing redesigned end-to-end (with the loan officer becoming an exception-handler and customer advisor) rather than separate AI tools per stage.
- *Specialty Pharma:* Indication-to-launch redesigned with AI in the loop at every gate (preclinical, IND, P1/2/3, regulatory, commercial readiness).
- *HR:* Employee lifecycle redesigned (acquire-develop-engage-transition) with AI making decisions, not just informing humans.

**Citation:** Slalom Blueprint for AI Transformation, leading consulting research.

---

## Pattern 3 — Governance from day one

**Principle:** Winners build governance into the foundation; laggards bolt it on after problems emerge.

**Generic statement:** Responsible AI governance — fairness, auditability, model risk, regulatory alignment — must be in place before scaled deployment, not after the first regulatory inquiry. The governance topics vary by domain; the principle is universal.

**Per-topic mapping examples:**
- *Mortgage Origination:* Fair lending model risk management, ECOA/Reg B compliance, adverse-action explainability built into the model lifecycle from day one.
- *Specialty Pharma:* GxP-aligned model validation, FDA AI/ML guidance compliance, patient-data privacy (HIPAA/GDPR) embedded in the platform.
- *HR:* EEOC compliance, adverse-impact testing, candidate-data privacy, transparent decisioning for any AI-influenced employment decision.

**Citation:** Slalom 2024 Executive Perspectives.

---

## Pattern 4 — 80%+ on function reshaping

**Principle:** Winners spend 80%+ of AI investment on reshaping how the function works; laggards spend it on productivity tools for individuals.

**Generic statement:** A copilot that makes one person 15% faster is a productivity tool. A workflow redesign that lets a function operate with a different shape (different roles, different cycle time, different cost structure) is a transformation. Winners do the latter.

**Per-topic mapping examples:**
- *Mortgage Origination:* Underwriter and processor roles redesigned around AI-led decisioning, not "underwriter with a copilot." Bottom-line: function operates with 30-50% fewer FTEs at 2x throughput.
- *Specialty Pharma:* Medical affairs reshaped around AI-driven HCP engagement instead of MSL-with-better-content-tools. Trial design teams reshaped around AI-led protocol synthesis.
- *HR:* Talent acquisition team operates as exception handlers on AI-driven sourcing/screening/scheduling. Learning function shifts from content-curators to skill-architects.

**Citation:** Leading consulting research, Slalom 2024 Executive Perspectives.

## How to use this in the dashboard

The "Why this is decisive" section presents all four patterns as a 2×2 or 1×4 grid. Each card has:
- The pattern number (01/02/03/04)
- The pattern name (e.g., "Shared platforms")
- A short tagline (e.g., "not siloed pilots")
- A 1-2 sentence generic statement
- A topic-specific callout box ("In [topic]:") with the concrete mapping

The headline above the grid: *"The four patterns that separate winners from laggards — applied to [topic]."*

The supporting paragraph leads with: *"Slalom's 2024 executive perspectives research, reinforced by leading consulting research on the 5% that capture material AI value, finds four patterns that separate winners from laggards."* (Cite both — no competitor names in client-facing content.)

A portfolio-proof callout below the grid asserts: *"This portfolio embodies all four patterns. [N]% workflow-level AI. [N]% on shared platforms. Governance from Phase 0. Function-reshaping concentration in Phase 2-3."* (Numbers from the actual portfolio mix.)
